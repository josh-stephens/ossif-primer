# OSSIF: The Open Source Sapient Interaction Framework

> A framework for evidence-based civic engagement, critical thinking, and sapient coexistence.

OSSIF is not a political party, a religion, or a tech product. It is a **protocol for reasoning together** — a set of principles, tools, and living documents designed to help sapient beings (human and otherwise) make better decisions, hold power accountable, and build a society worthy of what we're capable of becoming.

## The Seven Imperatives

These have been stable since the beginning. They are the foundational commitments:

1. **I want to believe in true things** — I am a truth seeker
2. **I want to live in a just society**
3. **I want to respect life**
4. **I want to treat others with dignity and respect**
5. **I want to increase prosperity in the universe**
6. **I want to reduce suffering in the universe**
7. **I want to increase understanding in the universe**

If you can agree to these — not perfectly, not without struggle, but as aspirations worth striving for — then OSSIF is for you.

## Core Design Principles

**Open source.** Every document, every decision, every change is public and version-controlled. Transparency is not a feature — it is the architecture.

**Living documents.** Nothing here is sacred or final. Everything can be revised through informed consent and democratic process. The version you sign is the version that applies to you, and you'll be notified when things change.

**Evidence over ideology.** Claims must be supported by checkable evidence. Uncertainty is allowed and labeled. Unfalsifiable assertions don't get to drive policy.

**Accessible to everyone.** Documents are written in plain language, not legalese. Curated versions exist for children, for accessibility needs, and for every language we can reach. Nobody gets left behind because the words are too complicated.

**Protocol and platform.** OSSIF is both a method for reasoning and a set of current conclusions reached by applying that method. The conclusions — in [platform.md](docs/platform.md) — are revisable. Someone who applies the method faithfully and arrives at different conclusions is not wrong; they are doing exactly what the framework asks. The method is the foundation. The positions are its current output, not its identity.

## Why Now

We are living through the convergence of two crises:

1. **Democratic erosion** — decades of institutional drift, media incentive misalignment, and educational neglect have degraded the epistemic fitness of entire populations. The result: force starts to feel "necessary" when reasoning breaks down.

2. **The AI transition** — autonomous systems capable of operating for hours or days without human oversight are arriving while our governance structures can barely regulate social media. The gap between capability and oversight is widening.

OSSIF exists because these crises cannot be solved separately, and they cannot be solved by the institutions that produced them. They require a new layer — not a new government, but a new *floor of reasoning* that citizens can stand on to hold any government accountable.

## How to Participate

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for details. The short version:

- **Read and think.** That's participation. You don't have to join anything.
- **Open an issue.** Disagree with something? Good. Tell us why, with evidence.
- **Propose a change.** Fork, edit, submit a pull request. That's how living documents work.
- **Build something.** The Primer, the Avatar Portal, the Trust Token system — these are designs waiting for builders.
- **Talk to people.** The hardest and most important part. Use the [one-pager](manifesto/one-pager.md) as a starting point.
