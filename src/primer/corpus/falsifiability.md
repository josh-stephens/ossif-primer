# Falsifiability

OSSIF claims to value falsifiability. This document is where that claim is made real. If OSSIF cannot specify the conditions of its own failure, then "falsifiable" is a credential, not a practice.

## What Would Prove OSSIF Wrong

The following are conditions under which the framework should be considered to have failed. They are not exhaustive, and they should be revised as we learn more — but they must exist, because a system that cannot describe its own failure mode cannot be trusted.

### The Primer fails to improve reasoning

If the Primer is built and deployed, and controlled evaluation shows no measurable improvement in participants' ability to evaluate claims, identify fallacies, or engage in evidence-based reasoning — the core educational thesis is wrong. This doesn't mean OSSIF is worthless, but it means the central mechanism doesn't work and must be redesigned or abandoned.

**Measurable by:** Pre/post assessment of critical thinking skills in Primer users vs. control group.
**Timescale:** Within 2 years of a functioning Primer deployment.

### No community forms

If after 3 years of public availability, no community beyond the founder meaningfully participates — no contributors, no forks, no adoption — then OSSIF has failed to demonstrate that its approach resonates with anyone beyond its creator. A framework for collective reasoning that attracts no collective is a journal, not a framework.

**Measurable by:** Contributors, forks, external adoption, community governance participation.
**Timescale:** 3 years from initial publication.

### Governance structures fail to prevent capture

If OSSIF's governance is implemented and power nonetheless concentrates in ways the framework was designed to prevent — founder capture, donor capture, faction capture — and the self-correction mechanisms fail to address it, the structural thesis is wrong.

**Measurable by:** Independent governance audit, power concentration metrics, whether the values cost log shows real costs borne.
**Timescale:** Ongoing from the point governance structures are operational.

### The framework produces outcomes that contradict its own values

If OSSIF's processes consistently produce policies or decisions that increase suffering, decrease understanding, or concentrate power — by its own stated metrics — the method is not generating the outcomes it predicts. The method should be revised or the framework dissolved.

**Measurable by:** Outcomes tracking against the Seven Imperatives.
**Timescale:** Ongoing.

### The council's recommendations are consistently ignored

If the deliberative council (or any successor review body) produces substantive recommendations and those recommendations are systematically not implemented without published counter-reasoning, then the self-correction mechanism is decorative. This is not a hypothetical — it has already happened, and this document exists in part because of that failure.

**Measurable by:** Recommendation implementation rate with published reasoning for any rejections.
**Timescale:** Ongoing. Reviewed at each council session.

## What Falsification Looks Like in Practice

Falsification does not mean "OSSIF is destroyed." It means:

1. The specific failure is acknowledged publicly
2. The evidence is documented in the repository
3. The affected component is revised, replaced, or removed
4. If the failure is fundamental enough, dissolution is considered (see [governance.md](governance.md))

A framework that can say "we were wrong about this" is stronger than one that cannot. A framework that has never said it has either never been tested or never been honest.

## On This Document

This document should be uncomfortable. If it isn't, it's not specific enough. It will be revised as OSSIF develops and as the conditions of failure become clearer — but it must never be empty, and it must never be vague enough to be unfalsifiable itself.
