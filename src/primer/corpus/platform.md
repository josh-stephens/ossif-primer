# Platform for Progress

These are concrete policy positions derived from OSSIF values. They are not partisan — they follow directly from the principles in [values.md](values.md). Each position is stated plainly, with the reasoning visible. Disagree with the reasoning? Open an issue.

## Voting Reform

- **Mandatory voting** with accessible mechanisms (by mail, early voting, automatic registration)
- **Independent redistricting commissions** to eliminate partisan gerrymandering
- **Approval-based or ranked-choice voting** to reduce spoiler effects and reward consensus candidates
- **Auditable paper trails** for all elections
- **National voting standards** for ballot access and nonpartisan administration

*Reasoning: If "We the People" is the foundation, then the mechanism for expressing that will must be robust, fair, and resistant to capture.*

## Education Reform

- **Renewed investment in public education** — funding, teacher compensation, infrastructure
- **Mandatory civic duty education** with critical thinking, evidence evaluation, and media literacy as core curricula
- **Regulation of homeschooling** to ensure minimum educational standards are met
- **Lifelong learning access** — community colleges and libraries as permanent educational infrastructure, not luxuries
- **Age-appropriate OSSIF Primer materials** available freely to all schools

*Reasoning: Education is the immune system of democracy. When it fails, everything downstream fails.*

## Constitutional and Government Reform

- **Codify many aspects of the Constitution** that currently rely on norms rather than enforceable rules
- **Enforceable Code of Conduct for the Supreme Court**
- **Abolish the filibuster** and other corruption-enabling procedural obstructions
- **Review and constrain presidential power creep** and constitutional loopholes
- **Abolish qualified immunity** and introduce nationwide law enforcement reform
- **Campaign finance reform**: reverse or cabin *Citizens United*, require real-time transparency, cap outside spending
- **Term limits** for all federal judges, including the Supreme Court
- **Fiscal responsibility legislation** with transparent spending reviews and ethical guidelines

*Reasoning: A Constitution written in the 18th century for a nation of 4 million must be adapted — not revered — for a nation of 330 million with nuclear weapons and artificial intelligence.*

## Separation of Church and State

- **Restoration and codification** of the wall between religious institutions and government
- **Remove religious language** from currency, pledges, and official documents
- **Tax religious institutions** like any other organization
- **No religious tests** for office, policy, or civic participation — enforced, not just stated
- Religiosity, like any unfalsifiable position, has no place in governance structures. Individuals may hold sacred beliefs freely; governments must operate on publicly defensible reasons.

*Reasoning: Any belief system becomes dangerous when it grants moral certainty to coercive power. The firewall keeps disagreement from becoming heresy.*

## Criminal Justice Reform

- **Abolish for-profit prison administration**
- **End qualified immunity** for law enforcement
- **Nationwide police reform** with independent civilian oversight
- **Sentencing reform** focused on rehabilitation over punishment
- **Equal application of law** — the same rules for officials, the wealthy, and ordinary citizens

*Reasoning: A system that profits from incarceration has an incentive to incarcerate. A system that shields officers from accountability has an incentive to abuse.*

## Economic Justice

- **Universal Basic Income** (or "Basic Dignity Income") — provide a floor below which no person can fall, funded by progressive taxation and automation dividends
- **Fair taxation**: progressive rates, close loopholes, tax capital gains as income, tax religious institutions
- **Antitrust enforcement** — break up monopolies, enforce price gouging prevention, scrutinize mergers that consolidate data or compute chokepoints
- **Paid family leave** — national fund for 6-month salary support to both parents
- **Affordable housing** as an investment in stability, not a commodity
- **Cap extreme wealth accumulation** at levels that prevent individuals from purchasing political outcomes

*Reasoning: Nobody should accumulate resources at enormous multiples of others at anyone's expense. Prosperity is not a zero-sum game, but hoarding is.*

## AI Governance

- **Natural language disclosure** — AI-generated content must be labeled as such
- **Pre-deployment assessments** and ongoing audits for high-impact AI systems
- **Explainability requirements** — if a system affects rights, liberty, or livelihoods, it must be understandable
- **Bias and fairness testing** — independent evaluation, not self-certification
- **Human review** required for consequential decisions (hiring, sentencing, benefits, medical)
- **Adopt and expand the NIST AI Risk Management Framework**
- **Tiered safeguards** for long-duration autonomous agents: audit logs, incident reporting, shutdown hooks
- **Open-source AI standards** for public-sector deployment

*Reasoning: Tools that can operate autonomously for hours or days require governance proportional to their capability. "Move fast and break things" is not an acceptable policy for systems that affect millions.*

## Environment

- **Enforce clean water and air standards**
- **Reinstate and strengthen habitat protections and endangered species protections**
- **Climate change action** — treat environmental stability as a first principle, not a line item
- **Intergenerational duty** — recognize a constitutional right to a habitable environment
- **Energy transition** with honest cost accounting and support for affected workers

*Reasoning: There is one planet. Stewardship is not optional.*

## Health Care

- **Universal health care** — access to medical care is a right, not a privilege
- **Eliminate profit incentives** that conflict with patient outcomes
- **Mental health parity** — treat mental health with the same urgency and funding as physical health
- **Bodily autonomy** — the right to make decisions about one's own body, including reproductive choices

*Reasoning: A society that allows people to die or go bankrupt from treatable conditions has failed a basic test of civilization.*

## Immigration

- **Legal immigration expansion** with streamlined processes
- **Asylum process reform** — humane, timely, and fair
- **Path to citizenship** for long-term residents and contributors
- **Reject dehumanization** of immigrants in policy and rhetoric

*Reasoning: A melting pot is our strength. People seeking safety and opportunity are not threats — they are us, displaced by circumstances.*

## Gun Safety

- **Aggressive gun safety reform** — universal background checks, red flag laws, ban on military-style weapons for civilian use
- **Close the gap between law and technology** — regulations that keep pace with weapon capabilities
- **Treat gun violence as the public health crisis it is**

*Reasoning: The right to safety of person — for children in schools, for people in grocery stores, for everyone — supersedes the right to own any weapon imaginable.*

## Media and Truth

- **Content provenance standards** — signed/watermarked media for public and political communications
- **Criminal penalties for deceptive synthetic political media**
- **Support for independent journalism** through grants and structural protections
- **Platform transparency** — algorithmic audits, data portability, interoperability mandates

*Reasoning: Democracy requires shared reality. When synthetic media can flood the zone, maintaining truth becomes infrastructure, not luxury.*

## A Note on This Platform

These positions will make some people uncomfortable. That's expected. Some will read this and see "liberal." Others will see positions that transcend any party — because fairness, accountability, and evidence are not owned by any tribe.

Every position above can be challenged. Open an issue. Bring evidence. If you can show that a position here increases suffering, decreases understanding, or violates the principles in [values.md](values.md), it should be revised. That's how living documents work.
