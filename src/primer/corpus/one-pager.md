# OSSIF in Two Minutes

## What is it?

OSSIF — the Open Source Sapient Interaction Framework — is a set of principles and tools for evidence-based civic engagement. It's designed to help people reason better together, hold power accountable, and prepare for a future shaped by artificial intelligence.

## What does it stand for?

Seven core commitments:

1. Believe in true things
2. Live in a just society
3. Respect life
4. Treat others with dignity
5. Increase prosperity
6. Reduce suffering
7. Increase understanding

If you can agree to strive for these — imperfectly, like everyone else — you're already aligned.

## What does it actually do?

Three things:

**Documents** — A living constitution, bill of rights, and policy platform written in plain language, version-controlled like software, and amendable through democratic process. Not scripture — a working agreement.

**Education** — The Primer: an adaptive learning tool that teaches critical thinking through personalized stories and dialogue. It meets you where you are and helps you evaluate claims, spot manipulation, and make better decisions. Think of it as an immune system against misinformation.

**Coordination** — Tools for structured civic conversation, transparent governance, and mutual aid. Not a party — a platform for citizens who want to engage with evidence rather than outrage.

## What makes it different?

- **Open source.** Every document, every algorithm, every decision is public.
- **No leader worship.** The founder has no special authority. The principles belong to everyone.
- **Includes AI.** Sapient AI systems get the same rights and obligations as anyone else. This isn't optional — it's a core design decision for the era we're entering.
- **Falsifiable.** If OSSIF doesn't work, the data will show it. Unlike ideologies, it's designed to be wrong and get corrected.

## Who is it for?

Everyone. Literally. The Primer adapts to children, adults, scientists, skeptics, and people who've never thought about politics. The documents are available in plain language, simplified versions, and (eventually) every language.

You don't have to be an activist. You don't have to join anything. Reading and thinking is participation.

## What can I do right now?

- **Read** the [values](../docs/values.md) and see if they resonate
- **Challenge** anything that doesn't hold up — open a GitHub issue with evidence
- **Share** this with someone who cares about the same things
- **Build** — if you can code, design, write, teach, or organize, there's work to do

## The pitch (if someone asks)

> "I'm involved with a civic reasoning project. It's open source — a set of principles and tools for evidence-based engagement. The goal is to help regular people make sense of current events, evaluate claims, and coordinate on practical action. No ideology required — just a commitment to treat people like people and be honest about evidence."

That's it. No recruitment funnel, no membership fee, no loyalty oath. Just better tools for thinking together.
